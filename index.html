About

I work on fault-tolerant quantum computation in Kenneth Brown's group at Duke University. My research centers on quantum error correction: Floquet and dynamical codes, transversal gates, and decoders for quantum LDPC codes.

PhD in Electrical & Computer Engineering, Duke University (2023–present) · MSc in Physics, Freie Universität Berlin · BA in Mathematics & Physics, Washington University in St. Louis

Software

  • stim-floquet Python

    Automated Floquet measurement-schedule synthesis, homology verification, and local detector discovery for CSS quantum error-correcting codes. Produces Stim circuits directly from parity-check matrices.

  • QECMinPoly.jl Julia

    Minimal-polynomial computation over GF(2) for CSS codes — reliable, scalable minpoly for the symplectic matrices arising in quantum error correction, built on Oscar.jl.

  • gate2code Python

    Re-implementation of the Campbell–Howard Synthillation framework for magic-state distillation and multi-qubit gate synthesis, from the original Mathematica supplement to a pip-installable library.
